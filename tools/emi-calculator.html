Frequently asked questions

What is EMI and how is it calculated? ▼

EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment) is the fixed amount you pay every month to repay a loan. It includes both principal and interest. The formula is: EMI = P × r × (1 + r)ⁿ ÷ [(1 + r)ⁿ – 1], where P is the principal, r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and n is the tenure in months. Early payments are mostly interest; later payments shift toward principal.

What is an amortization schedule? ▼

An amortization schedule is a complete table of every loan payment, broken down month by month. It shows the opening balance, your EMI, how much goes toward interest, how much reduces the principal, and the closing balance. It's useful for understanding exactly how your debt decreases over time and how much interest you're paying in any given month.

Does this calculator work for home loans, car loans, and personal loans? ▼

Yes. The EMI formula is universal across all loan types — home loans, auto loans, personal loans, education loans, and business loans. The only difference is the interest rate and tenure. Just enter the numbers from your loan offer and the calculator will give you accurate results.

Why does a longer tenure lower my EMI but increase total interest? ▼

A longer tenure spreads the principal over more months, so each payment is smaller. But since interest accrues on the outstanding balance every month, you end up paying interest for a longer period. The total interest can be significantly higher — often more than the principal itself for very long loans. A shorter tenure means higher EMIs but saves you money overall.

Is this calculator accurate for all currencies? ▼

Yes. The mathematical formula is currency-agnostic — select your currency from the dropdown and the calculator will display results with the correct symbol. The computation is identical regardless of currency. For currencies like JPY or NPR that typically don't use decimal places, amounts are rounded to the nearest whole unit.
