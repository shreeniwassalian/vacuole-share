Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate a discount percentage? ▼

Discount % = ((Original Price − Sale Price) ÷ Original Price) × 100. For example, if something originally costs $200 and is on sale for $150, the discount is ((200 − 150) ÷ 200) × 100 = 25% off. Use the Reverse Calculator above to compute this automatically.

How do I find the final price after a discount? ▼

Final Price = Original Price × (1 − Discount% ÷ 100). So a 30% discount on $500 gives $500 × 0.70 = $350. The calculator shows this instantly alongside the amount saved.

What's the difference between discount % and savings %? ▼

They're the same thing in the context of a single item — both express how much of the original price you're saving. The term "discount %" is used by the seller, while "savings %" is how shoppers often think about it. Both equal (Amount Saved ÷ Original Price) × 100.

Can I use this for stacked or multiple discounts? ▼

For stacked discounts (e.g. 20% off, then an extra 10% off), you can apply them in steps: calculate the price after the first discount, then use that as the new original price for the second discount. Two successive discounts are never the same as adding the percentages together — 20% + 10% off is not 30% off.

Does this work with any currency? ▼

Yes. Select your currency from the dropdown and all values will display with the correct symbol. The math is identical regardless of currency — percentages and ratios are universal.
